## Types of Sports Bets Covered

  

Trophies Sports Picks covers virtually every type of sports wager you can imagine:

  

### Primary Bet Types

  

**Moneyline**: Simple win/loss bets where you pick the outright winner. No point spread involved, making these perfect for beginners.

  

**Point Spread**: Bets against the handicap line set by sportsbooks. The favorite must win by more than the spread, while the underdog can lose by less than the spread and still cover.

  

**Over/Under (Totals)**: Wagers on whether the combined score will be over or under the posted total. These bets focus on offensive and defensive matchups rather than game outcomes.

  

**Parlay**: Multiple bets combined into one wager for higher payouts. All selections must win for the parlay to cash, making these high-risk, high-reward bets.

  

### Advanced Betting Options

  

**Teaser Bets**: Modified parlays where you get extra points on each selection in exchange for reduced payouts. Popular for football and basketball betting.

  

**Proposition Bets**: Specific outcome wagers not tied to the final score. Examples include player performances, first touchdown scorer, or total rebounds.

  

**Futures Bets**: Long-term wagers on season outcomes like championship winners or award recipients. These offer great value when placed early.

  

**Live Betting**: Real-time wagering during games as odds shift based on game flow. Perfect for capitalizing on momentum changes.

  

**Round Robin Bets**: Multiple smaller parlays created from a larger group of selections, providing more ways to win than traditional parlays.

  

### Specialized Wagers

  

**Middle Bets**: Advanced strategy where you bet both sides of a game at different point spreads, creating a "middle" range where both bets can win.

  

**Reverse Forecast Bets**: Predicting the exact order of finish for competitors, commonly used in racing and motorsports.

  

The diversity of bet types means there's always action available, regardless of your preferred sport or risk tolerance.

## Sports Betting Legal Landscape

  

Before joining any sports betting service, it's crucial to understand the legal landscape in your area.

  

### United States Legal Framework

  

Sports betting was largely illegal under the Professional and Amateur Sports Protection Act of 1992 (PASPA), which prohibited states from authorizing legal sports betting.

  

Only Delaware, Montana, Nevada, and Oregon were grandfathered in with existing frameworks.

  

The Supreme Court struck down PASPA in Murphy v. National Collegiate Athletic Association in 2018, opening the door for state-by-state legalization.

  

### Current Legal Status

  

As of January 2024:

-   **37 states, Washington D.C., and Puerto Rico** have operational legalized sports betting
-   **North Carolina** has legalized sports betting with operations beginning later in 2024
-   **30 states, Washington D.C., and Puerto Rico** offer online sports betting
-   **8 states** only allow betting through physical sportsbooks

  

Since the Supreme Court's PASPA decision, over $20 billion has been spent on legal sports betting in the United States as of May 2020.
